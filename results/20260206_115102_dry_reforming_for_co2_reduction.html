Critical Issues & Risks

Economic Viability

The 60% higher energy requirement compared to steam reforming creates significant operational cost challenges. Combined with high capital expenditure for specialized reactors, DRM currently lacks cost competitiveness without subsidies or high carbon pricing.

Technical Reliability

Catalyst coking remains a critical issue. Despite laboratory advances, industrial-scale durability is unproven. Regeneration cycles introduce operational complexity and downtime risks that could undermine economic feasibility.

Integration Barriers

DRM requires specific feedstock conditions (high-purity CO‚ÇÇ and CH‚ÇÑ streams) and produces syngas that may not be directly compatible with existing chemical processes, creating significant infrastructure challenges.

Competitive Landscape

Established alternatives like steam methane reforming and emerging technologies like green hydrogen via electrolysis are receiving substantial investment, potentially achieving scale and cost reductions faster than DRM.
